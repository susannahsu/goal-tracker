Goal Tracking Platform — Quick Start GuideFor pilot team managers and goal owners. Takes ~5 minutes to read.

What This Is

A lightweight tool for tracking company and team goals in one place. It connects your team's work to Fluidstack's strategic pillars so everyone can see how things ladder up — and where things stand.
You're not writing essays here. You're keeping a shared scoreboard current.


Two Views

Goals Table

The default view. Shows all goals in a flat table, split into two sections:

* Strategy (top section): Mission, strategic position, and strategic pillars. These are set by leadership — you don't need to edit these.
* Team Goals (bottom section): This is where your team's goals live. Each row shows the goal, which pillar it ladders to, the owning team, the named owner, target date, and current status.

Waterfall View

A visual hierarchy showing how everything connects — mission at the top, pillars in the middle, team goals at the bottom. Useful for seeing the big picture and checking that your goals actually ladder to something strategic.

Adding a Goal

Click + ADD GOAL (top right). You'll see a form with these fields:

	Select "Team Goal" for your team's goals. Leave the strategic levels alone.

Goal Name
	A clear, specific statement of what you're trying to achieve. Not a task — a goal. (See "Writing Good Goals" below.)

	Which strategic pillar does this ladder to? Pick the most relevant one. If it doesn't ladder to any pillar, reconsider whether it belongs here.

	The single person accountable for this goal. Not a team — a name.

	When this should be done or evaluated.

Hit Save Goal and it appears in both views.


Updating Goals (Your Main Job)

Every other Monday by EOD, update the status of your goals. That's it.

Status options:

* Not Started — Work hasn't begun yet. Fine for future-dated goals.
* On Track — Progress is happening as expected. No intervention needed.
* At Risk — Something could derail this. Maybe a dependency, resource issue, or timeline concern. Flag it early.
* Behind — We've missed a milestone or are clearly off-pace. Needs attention.
* Completed — Done. Ship it.

What a good update looks like:

Change the status dropdown if anything shifted. That's the minimum. If you moved from "On Track" to "At Risk," a one-sentence reason is helpful ("blocked on vendor contract"). Don't write a paragraph.

What a bad update looks like:

Leaving everything as "On Track" for 6 weeks when it clearly isn't. If you're not sure, "At Risk" is almost always the more honest and useful call.

Writing Good Goals

A goal is not a task. "Set up CI/CD pipeline" is a task. "Reduce deployment time from 2 hours to 15 minutes by Q2" is a goal.

A few tests for whether your goal is well-written:

1. Can you tell if it's done? If the finish line is vague, sharpen it.
2. Does it ladder to a pillar? If not, it might be important work, but it doesn't go in this tool.
3. Is there one owner? "The team" is not an owner. Pick the person who'll update this.
4. Does it have a date? Open-ended goals are aspirations, not goals.

FAQ

Do I need to update goals that haven't started? 
No. Leave them as "Not Started" until work actually begins. Don't update for the sake of updating.

Can I delete a goal? 
Yes, use the trash icon on the right side of any row to delete.

What if my goal doesn't fit any strategic pillar? 
Either the goal needs reframing, or there's a missing pillar. Don't force-fit it.

What if a goal becomes irrelevant mid-cycle? 
Flag it. Don't just leave it sitting there as "Not Started" forever. Deprecating a goal with a reason is better than a graveyard of stale entries.
